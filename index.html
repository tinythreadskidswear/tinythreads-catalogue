📏 Size Guide
How to measure: Measure chest circumference (fullest part) and height while standing straight. Round up to the next size if between two sizes.
Size Tag Age Height (cm) Chest (cm) Weight (kg)
3-4Y 3-4 yrs 94-104 53-55 14-16
5-6Y 5-6 yrs 104-116 55-58 16-20
7-8Y 7-8 yrs 116-128 58-62 20-25
9-10Y 9-10 yrs 128-138 62-66 25-30
11-12Y 11-12 yrs 138-148 66-70 30-36
13-14Y 13-14 yrs 148-158 70-74 36-44
💡For traditional wear like kurtas, we recommend going one size up for a relaxed fit. If between sizes, always choose the larger size.
How to measure: Measure chest (fullest part), waist (natural waistline) and height. For frocks and lehengas, waist + height matters most.
Size Tag Age Height (cm) Chest (cm) Waist (cm)
3-4Y 3-4 yrs 94-104 52-54 49-51
5-6Y 5-6 yrs 104-116 54-57 51-53
7-8Y 7-8 yrs 116-128 57-61 53-56
9-10Y 9-10 yrs 128-138 61-65 56-59
11-12Y 11-12 yrs 138-148 65-69 59-62
13-14Y 13-14 yrs 148-158 69-73 62-65
💡For lehengas and sharara sets, measure waist carefully. Frocks are sized primarily by height -- go one size up for a longer, flowy fit.
How to measure: Babies grow fast! Measure weight and height fresh before ordering. All baby garments have generous seam allowances.
Size Tag Age Height (cm) Weight (kg) Chest (cm)
0-3M 0-3 months 50-62 3-6 38-42
3-6M 3-6 months 62-68 6-8 42-44
6-9M 6-9 months 68-74 8-10 44-46

12-18M 12-18 months 80-86 11-13 48-50
18-24M 18-24 months 86-92 13-14 50-52
2-3Y 2-3 years 92-98 14-15 52-54
💡Babies grow 3-4 cm per month in the first year. Buying one size ahead is wise -- our soft fabrics are comfortable even in a slightly larger fit.

Our story Tiny Threads Kidswear with craft, care and community
40 years of boutique and stitching experience

Crafted with experience. Growing with purpose.

Uma Gangrade brings four decades of hands-on boutique work, garment stitching and a deep understanding of what families value in children's clothing.

Today, she is building Tiny Threads to help women in local communities become independent kidswear reselling partners, creating flexible income opportunities through trusted neighbourhood relationships.
